About

CV LinkedIn Scholar GitHub

I am a clinical bioinformatics scientist dedicated to developing and implementing cutting-edge bioinformatics solutions that streamline clinical workflows and improve diagnostic accuracy. My expertise lies in leveraging machine learning, digital pathology, and augmented artificial intelligence to advance precision medicine and enhance patient care.

Currently, I lead team and project management of data science and bioinformatics projects in the Department of Pathology and Laboratory Medicine and Center for Personalized Medicine at Children's Hospital Los Angeles. My work integrates molecular diagnostics, computational pathology, and translational discovery to improve diagnostic precision for CNS tumors and constitutional genetic disorders.

I envision a healthcare landscape where augmented AI solutions play a pivotal role in revolutionizing patient care. By leveraging the power of AI and machine learning, we can develop systems that assist clinicians in diagnosing complex diseases, predicting patient outcomes, and personalizing treatment plans.

Projects

Selected work in clinical AI, genomics, and translational informatics.

Liquid Biopsy cfDNA Medulloblastoma

cfDNA Fragmentomics for Medulloblastoma Subtyping

Problem: Invasive tissue biopsies are required for CNS tumor molecular subtyping, limiting diagnostic options.

Solution: Developed a cfDNA fragmentomics framework that identifies genome-wide fragmentation signatures in cerebrospinal fluid predictive of medulloblastoma molecular subtypes, achieving near-perfect classification accuracy (AUC = 0.94).

Role: First author, lead developer

Paper (npj Precis. Onc.) GitHub
Methylation MLOps Clinical

Methylation Array Analysis (MAA) Pipeline

Problem: Manual classification of CNS tumors is time-consuming and inconsistent across institutions.

Solution: Developed an MLOps framework for DNA methylation-based CNS tumor classification with continuous training and validation pipelines, classifying over 100 cancer types. Used weekly by CHLA's integrated neuro-oncology tumor board.

Role: Lead developer and clinical integration

Preprint
Multi-omics Subtyping Algorithm

iSubGen: Integrative Disease Subtyping

Problem: Disease subtyping often relies on single data modalities, missing insights from multi-modal data.

Solution: Contributed to iSubGen, an integrative subtyping algorithm that leverages pairwise similarity across genomic, transcriptomic, proteomic, and clinical data to identify disease subtypes even with incomplete data.

Role: Contributor

Paper (Cell Rep Methods)
CNV Analysis Exome Optical Genome Mapping

Clinical Genomics & CNV Analysis

Problem: Copy-number variants are often missed or misclassified in standard clinical exome sequencing workflows.

Solution: Co-led studies demonstrating that concurrent CNV analysis during exome sequencing increases diagnostic yield, and that optical genome mapping improves interpretation of complex CNVs.

Role: Co-lead, methods development

Paper (npj Genom. Med.)
