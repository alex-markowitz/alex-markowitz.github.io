About

CV LinkedIn Scholar GitHub

I am a clinical bioinformatics scientist specializing in machine learning, clinical informatics, and translational research. My work focuses on developing AI-driven tools that enhance diagnostic workflows and improve patient outcomes in pediatric medicine.

With expertise in quantitative sciences, biophysics, and computational biology, I design and implement production-grade systems for clinical diagnostics. My research integrates cutting-edge data-driven approaches with biomedical sciences to drive innovation in precision medicine.

I work at the intersection of AI, pathology, and genomics to develop next-generation tools for personalized diagnostics, with a focus on pediatric oncology and rare disease.

Projects

Selected work in clinical AI, genomics, and translational informatics.

Methylation ML Pipeline Clinical

Methylation Array Analysis (MAA) Pipeline

Problem: Manual classification of CNS tumors is time-consuming and inconsistent across institutions.

Solution: Developed an automated pipeline leveraging machine learning for classifying over 100 cancer types based on DNA methylation patterns, now used in clinical workflows at CHLA.

Role: Lead developer and clinical integration

Preprint
AI/ML Oncology Risk Stratification

Tumor Classification & Risk Stratification

Problem: Pediatric tumor prognosis relies on limited clinical markers, missing molecular insights.

Solution: AI-driven models integrating genomic and clinical data for improved tumor classification and patient risk stratification.

Role: Model development and validation

Paper
Neuroscience Biophysics Electrophysiology

Biophysical Encoding of Sound Intensity

Problem: The mechanisms by which auditory neurons encode sound intensity across a wide dynamic range were poorly understood.

Solution: Characterized how variations in neuronal biophysics contribute to auditory intensity coding through patch-clamp electrophysiology.

Role: Lead researcher (PhD thesis)
